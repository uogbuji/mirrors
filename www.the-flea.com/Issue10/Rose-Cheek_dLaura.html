Rose-Cheekt Lawra

by Thomas Campion

  Rose-cheekt Lawra, come, 
Sing thou smoothly with thy beawty’s 
Silent musick, either other 
      Sweetely gracing. 
 
  Lovely forms do flowe         
From concent devinely framed: 
Heav’n is musick, and thy beawties 
      Birth is heavenly. 
 
  These dull notes we sing 
Discords neede for helps to grace them;  
Only beawty purely loving 
      Knowes no discord; 
 
  But still mooves delight, 
Like cleare springs reneu’d by flowing, 
Ever perfect, ever in them-  
      selves eternall.

 

From Observations in the Art of English Poesie (1602)
10
November, 2010
Thomas Campion hath studied at Cambridge, Gray’s Inn and Caen in Normandy. He hath publish’d verse alongside Sir Philip Sidney, and, well knowne as a Musician of great accomplishment, hath compos’d many fyne divers Ayres and Ditties, especially for the Lute. He currently laboureth upon the Composition of his Booke, A New Way of Making Fowre Parts in Counterpoint By a Most Familiar and Infallible Rule, which he trusteth to see printed within the next decade.
