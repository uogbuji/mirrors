Recess

by Jennifer Reeser

Behind the floating bathroom wall,
Something has gone rotten,
Unreachable, remote, sub-tub,
Defunct but not forgotten.
 
All day odor plays upstairs,
Then the scent will drift
Downstairs to the dining room
To work an evening shift,
 
Where it sees us slice our meat
Sliver by foul sliver—
How ironic, to obstruct
The taste of rot with liver!


12
January, 2011
Madame Jennifer Reeser, authoress of twain poetical collections, An Alabaster Flask and Winterproof, in modern publication by the firm Word Press, hath as well created and addressed to the English-speaking world the Shakespearean series ‘Sonnets from the Dark Lady’. She is a translatorix of poetical works from both the French and Russian quills. In addition, Milady is the mistress of a white house of one marital master, the Sire of her five juvenile issue; which white house nestleth among the mystic marshes and cypress knees of Frankish, catholique southern Louisiana, United States of America.
